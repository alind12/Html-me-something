THINGS ABOUT NEW BADEN

FOOD

A Fine Swine BBQ
Good Ol' Days
El Indio
Margaritas Mexican Restaurant

New Baden is ever expanding in the choices of places to eat at. With the newest being A Fine Swine BBQ. A well loved BBQ place with a local owner. David Stidham, has won man awards in barbecue competitions. A Fine Swine is becoming the local destination for great eats. New Baden also has Mexican and Chinese cuisine, with several more choices, such as Mac Donalds, a Hardee's. Two gas stations Kokomos and Casey's also serve hot food.

EVENTS

St. George Parish Picnic
American legion Meat Shoot

Halloween Cemetery Walk

There is no shortage of cultural enrichment, mostly outside and interacting with others. Some main events are the June Jamboree, with a 5k run/walk, live music, rides and games. There is also a car motorcycle show, a mud bog. There is also food and drink for all ages. The Spring/fall City Wide Yard Sale is another get out and meet others event. During this event most of the town is selling there unused/unwanted items. The Zion Church has food, as do many of the locals and there children, with a large vendor selection at the Legion Hall. The Chili Cook off with the Cupcake Wars is a Huge hit in the fall. There is also a car show during this event with live music and vendors with hand made crafts. At the end of the night there is a bonfire for all to enjoy.

LANDMARKS

St. George Catholic Church
Berkemann's Baker's Dozen
Good Ol' Days Restaurant
New Baden Community Park

With St. George Church rising high near the center of the village it is hard not to use it as a land mark, it is a beautiful structure in Gothic style, built of solid brick. Located on the main street through the village is Berkemann's Baker's Dozen building built in 1903 and known as Hill's Bakery and operated by William Hill. Now owned and operated by local Paul Berkemann who serves up "Paul's Delights " in the fall among other amazing baked goods. Just Next to Village Hall you have Good Ol' Days Restaurant, which is the oldest restaurant in Clinton County. Serving a wide varity of home cooked foods. On the East end of the main street is the New Baden Community Park. 41 acres of outdoor fun, the park has a swimming pool, tennis courts, ball diamonds, soccer field, hockey rink, skate-park, asphalt walking path (1-mile in length), play-ground equipment, sand volleyball court, basketball court, and picnic facilities. Major events such as June Jamboree and The Chili Cook Off are held here in the park.

NEAR BY LOCATIONS

Trenton
Scott Air Force Base
Mascoutah

Trenton is home to the second Dairy King location, well known to the locals for Ski soda and fresh cooked foods. Trenton is also the location of a fort built in 1810 by Nathaniel Journey. The fort was to serve as a base for military efforts against the Native Americans. Close by is the Historic Scott Air Force Base, Created in 1917 after the US entered WWI. At the time of creation was called Scott Field, as this is before the creation of the Air Force. Now home to the Air Mobility Command supporting military operations around the world. Mascoutah, named for the Mascoutens, a tribe of the Illinois Indians. With a close proximity to Scott AFB it is ever growing and progressing.

HISTORY

Founded in 1855
Mostly German Desent
Devestating Tornadoes

Founded in 1855, New Baden is named after the historical territory of Baden, Germany. The town is mostly German in decent and names come from locations they were near in Germany, when the came to America. Many of the last names indicate that German heritage. New Baden was a relitively small village until struck by a devastating tornado on May 27, 1896. Neighboring communities contributed to the rebuilding, creating a larger, more modern town which began to thrive. The village was again struck by a devistating tornado on December 2, 1982 with 1 individual passing in the incident.
